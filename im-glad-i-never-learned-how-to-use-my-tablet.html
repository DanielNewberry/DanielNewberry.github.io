I'm Glad I Never Learned How to Use My Tablet

05 Aug 2024

In a world full of notifications and notification lights, not learning how to fully use my tablet has one of the best decisions I have ever made.

I was originally looking at getting an iPad but I couldn't justify it as an investment. Too high a cost for a device that does everything my Macbook and iPhone can do.

A month ago I bought a (second hand) Onyx Boox Nova 3 after finding one for a quarter of the price of a new iPad. I got it with the intention of using it just to read, write notes and annotate PDFs. But it can do a lot more than that. It's a full Android tablet that can browse the web, receive notifications, run apps, send emails, play games and a whole lot more.

But I have just kept it for reading and writing.

By not diving into the tablet's full functionality, I've eliminated a lot of distractions. No notifications, no internet rabbit holes - just me and my notes. This has massively improved my focus. Without the temptation to check social media or browse the web (browsing on an e-ink device is horrendous anyway), I have been able to focus on my work.

I think for me this tablet combines the best of both worlds: the feel of writing by hand and the convenience of digital storage. I can search through my notes, sync them across devices and access them anywhere. It’s like having a never-ending notebook that magically organises everything for me.

By not fully utilising my most of the capabilities, I've adopted a more mindful approach to this piece of technology. I’ve defined its role in my life, giving me a sense of control and purpose. Technology should serve us, not overwhelm us.

If you’re overwhelmed by digital noise, try simplifying your tech use. You might find that doing less helps you achieve more.
