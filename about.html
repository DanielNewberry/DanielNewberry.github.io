About

I'm a PhD Candidate and Researcher at Newcastle University Business School, currently focusing on sustainability, entrepreneurship, the environment and SMEs in the UK.

I started my PhD in September 2021 after completing an MA in Arts, Business and Creativity and a BA (Hons) in Music from Newcastle University.

During my undergraduate studies, I founded a record label in Newcastle that realeased music digitally and on cassette tapes. I later started a creative media consultancy, providing marketing and communications support for SMEs in the North East of England.

While running both businesses, I pursued my MA to explore the connection between the arts, creative industries and business. My dissertation sparked an interest in how environmental sustainability impacts small businesses, leading to my current PhD research.

Policy

In 2024, I was awarded a Fellowship for the Newcastle University Policy Academy. This fellowship will help me connect my research with practical policy-making in areas such as sustainable business development and environmental sustainability. Through this, I am engaging in regional and national policy discussions, using my research to shape policies that support sustainable business practices and development.

My focus is on collaborating with policymakers, industry leaders, academics and other stakeholders to advocate for a supportive environment for SMEs and micro-businesses, particularly in the North East of England. Integrating insights from my PhD research, I aim to contribute to evidence-based policies that promote environmental sustainability and economic growth.

Other

Since 2020 I have been a Fellow of the Royal Society of Arts (FRSA). Through this I have been involved in RSA projects that deliver social benefit, including work on a Universal Basic Income (UBI) and the development of Creative Corridors.
